Simple Ogbono Recipe


Ogbono soup (Draw Soup) is a Nigerian dish made with ground dry ogbono seeds. Ogbono seeds are originated and were first grown in Southern Nigeria with considerable local variation. Also, called Ogbolo by some Nigerians.
Ogbono soup (Ogbolo soup) is a rich,
nutrition-packed Nigerian soup made from ogbono seeds (ground African mango seeds), palm oil, assorted meats, and traditional spices.


Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 30minutes
  • Preparation: 15minutes
  • Cooking: 15minutes

Ingredients

  • Handfuls Ogbono Seeds
  • Palm Oil (MODERN when not available use peanut oil or groundnut oil)
  • Big seasoning cubes
  • Tablespoons ground crayfish
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Crayfish
  • Onion MODERN and(Optional)
  • Pumpkin leaf ugu
  • Meat (assorted, locust beans, beef e.t.c as you like it, BUSH MEAT Bush meat)
  • Oporoko
  • Fish (any type as you like it)
  • E.T.C (Additional Fillings) where neccessary


Instructions

  1. In an empty pot on low heat mix palm oil and ogbono to form a smooth paste
  2. Add stock, let it simmer and boil till it begins to bubble and draw
  3. Add meat, Pomo, and dry fish then season with spices. Let it all boil for 15 minutes
  4. Garnish soup with chopped Pumpkin and let it simmer for 2 minutes
  5. ENJOY: Serve hot(or as required) with any Nigerian swallow (Eba, Santana, Apku, etc) if needed...
